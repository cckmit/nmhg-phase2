INTRODUCTION

A retailed truck will have a set of warranties associated with it. The warranties could be standard warranties that come along with the truck, policy warranties that are provided by the OEM as policy or they could be extended warranties that are purchased by the end consumer. If a failure occurs on such an truck, and it is covered by the applicable warranty, the service provider (or any authorized service personnel) can perform the necessary service and re-claim the expenses, parts, labor and more, from the OEM. This is achieved by filing a warranty claim. In brief, the service provider specifies details regarding the expenses incurred and claims them.

 

The Warranty Management application allows you, as a service provider, to file claims against the repair/replacement made on a warranted truck. You can submit and claim the charges incurred for replaced or repaired parts, labor, travel etc. from the manufacturer of the truck.

 

The Warranty Management application allows you to:

  • Submit claims against applicable warranty.

  • Track claims through their life cycle.

  • Maintain information about your stock and retailed inventories.

  • Register warranties for each inventory in your stock.

  • Submit and track replaced parts that need to be returned to the OEM.

  • Generate truck transfer report when a retailed truck is sold from one customer to another.

The Warranty Management application thus allows you to manage various warranty claims in an easy and efficient manner.
