MAJOR COMPONENT REGISTRATION

Truck may consist of various parts which have their own warranty coverage. In case of serialized parts, you will have to register the component under Register Major Component.

 

To register the major component:

  • Go to the Home page, Click on Register Major Component option under Go To. A Register Major Component page will open.

  • Dealer: This is a read-only field and displays the name of your dealership.

  • Component Serial Number: Enter the serial number of the component you want to register. This number will uniquely identify the part/truck and will also determine the warranty coverage associated with it.

  • Component Part Number: Select the part number of the component from the drop down list.

  • Description: A short description of the component auto-populates.

  • Shipment Date: Use the  option to specify the date on which the part was shipped for delivery. You can also type the date manually in this field.

  • Date of Delivery: Use the  option to specify the date on which the part was delivered. You can also type the date manually in this field.

  • Installer: An installer may or may not be certified in the system.

  • Certified installer: Select the radio button ‘Dealer/Certified Installer’. All certified installers present in the system will appear in the drop-down.

  • Non-certified installer: Select the radio button ‘Non-Certified Installer’. The address book of the Dealer filing Delivery Report is shown to choose non-certified installer information.

  • Unit serial number: Select ‘Unit serial number’ to enter the inventory present in the system.

Note: Only Retail inventories can be selected. Unit serial number can be used only for parts claim having ‘Part installed on serialized host machine’.

  • Stand-Alone Parts: If you do not have the inventory present in the system, select Stand-Alone Parts. Select the customer information from the end customer list.

Note: User can either choose Unit Serial Number or Stand-alone Parts but not both of them. Stand-alone part serial number can be used for other three types of claim ‘Part installed on non-serialized host machine’, ‘Part installed on competitor model’, and ‘Part not installed’.

  • Click Submit. The policy details window will pop-up. Click Submit to confirm or click Edit if you want to edit any information under the Register Major Component page.

To view the major component details for an truck,

  • Go to the Home page, click on Inventory link under Quick Search. A new page opens up with details for the major component registered including the claim and transaction history for the truck. If you know the serial number of the major component, click on Major Component link under Quick Search to see all the details pertaining to that major component including the claim history and warranty coverage for that component.

  • Scroll to the Major Components section. You can view a list of all registered major components with details.

  • Click on View Components link on the right-hand side. You can view the history of any listed major component. Click on History to see if any claim has been filed on that major component.

The Warranty Management application facilitates automatic registration and transfer of coverage on parts that are registered as major component. All major components have warranty coverage attached to it. Thus, when the filed claim is accepted and the Bill of Material (BOM) is updated, the registration and coverage is automatically transferred from the removed part to the installed part. The installed part will now pull in all the details that were earlier applicable to the removed part. You will not find any details for the removed part any longer.
