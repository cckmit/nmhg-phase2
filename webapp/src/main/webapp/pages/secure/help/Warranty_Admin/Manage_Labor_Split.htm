MANAGE LABOR SPLIT

Labor rates are set up by the Warranty Administrator for a dealer before the dealer can file a warranty claim. Thus, while filing of a warranty claim, the dealer mentions the servicing information in terms of the job code that was applied to the claim. The system fetches the pre-defined number of hours that was set up for this job code. In addition to the normal hours spent on the job, the dealer can specify any additional hours that were spent on the job. The claim amount will take these additional hours into account while calculating the cost of the claim.

Now, sometimes the repair/service may require labor to work beyond the normal working hours and hence eligible for overtime pay. These overtime charges need to be setup into the system as a part of the labor rate configuration. This division of labor hours is known as labor split.

 

The labor split is an optional utility which can be enabled or disabled for a dealer. If enabled, the labor split field is displayed for the dealer under the Service Information section while filing the claim.

 

To enable the labor split utility,

  • Click on Manage Business Configurations tab under the Warranty Admin tab on the left-hand pane.

  • Click on Claim Input Parameters under Claims section in the Manage Business Configurations page.

  • Select the radio button ‘Yes’ for “Enable OverTime Labor Split”.

  • Click Update.

You can also create different labor splits as per your requirement.

 

To create a labor split,

  • Click on Manage Labor Split tab under the Warranty Admin tab on the left-hand pane.

  • Click on  button in the listing toolbar.

  • Enter the Labor Split Name and rate under Multiplication Value.

  • Click Submit.

The new labor split will be listed on the Manage Labor Split page. You can also edit the labor split by clicking on the required labor split listed on the Manage Labor Split page. Once you update the Multiplication Value, click Update to save the new value.

Now, when you enter the overtime hours under Labor Split section and validate the claim, you will see a difference in amount being filed for standard and overtime hours.
