The Warranty Management system allows a service provider to re-appeal for a claim that was partially accepted or completely denied by you. When a dealer appeals for a claim, the claim will appear in your Appeals folder.

 

To view all claims appealed by service providers, click on the Claims tab on the left-hand pane. The tab will open to reveal the folders under it. Click on the Appeals folder. On this page you can see the Appeals table that lists all claims marked appeal by the dealer. For further information on navigating through the table, see Lists.

 

You can see the following details for each claim in the Appealed table:

  • Claim Number: Reference number of the claim.

  • Claim Type: Kind of claim. A claim can be of type machine, parts or campaign.

  • Dealer: Name of the service provider who filed this claim.

  • Fault Location: Failure code associated with this claim.

  • Causal Part: Part serial number or part number of the part which caused the failure in the equipment.

  • Failure Date: Date when the equipment failed.

  • Repair Date: Date when the equipment was repaired.

You can view the details of any claim by clicking on it. Double clicking on a claim will open it in a new page, <claim number>. The claim detail page for a claim in this folder is divided into various sections for ease of navigation: Claim History, Claim Header, Equipment Details, Failure Information, Service Information, Components Replaced, Miscellaneous, Manage Documents, Payment Details and Actions. The fields under some sections on this page may be hidden. To view the fields, click on the section header. This will expand the section showing all fields under it. Likewise, clicking on the section header of an expanded section collapses it hiding all fields under it.

 

The reasons given by the dealer for appealing this claim will be shown as the first entry in the Claim History section. The claim will now behave exactly like a claim in your Processor Review folder. You can add/edit any details in this claim. For further information on functioning of claims in this folder, click here.
