policy management

A warranty policy is a deed that assures a product is reliable and free from known defects. It also asserts that the product will be repaired or the defective parts will be replaced without any charge and reimbursement of the expenses incurred for various services involved in a warranted repair will be made within a given time limit and under certain conditions. The services and charges covered by a policy are decisive factors in payment of a claim submitted on that policy.

 

Warranty policies dictate the terms of warranty coverage and are the decisive factor for a claim getting paid or not. The manufacturer provides a warranty for its products. A warranty policy can be categorized into three types – standard, extended or goodwill.

  • A standard OEM warranty, in general, is a warranty coverage provided by the OEM along with the product.

  • An extended warranty, on the other hand, is not given along with the product. This warranty can be bought by an end consumer as an insurance against future damage that might happen.

  • A goodwill policy is generally a one-time use policy used by the authorized OEM sales or service personnel and is something that is not a norm. It is an exceptional grant of policy to specific product or a specific customer.
