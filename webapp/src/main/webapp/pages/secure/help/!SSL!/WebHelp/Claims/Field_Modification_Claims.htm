FIELD MODIFICATION Claim

Field Modification is a type of claim that can be submitted when a service campaign has been performed on an equipment, by you. A service campaign is a service initiated by the manufacturer where a specific set of equipment in the field is marked to be serviced even without the failure.

 

To create and submit a field modification claim:

Go to the Home page, click on Create Claim option under Go To, a new page, New Claim is shown. You are now on Page 1 of 2 on this page. In the Claim Type drop-down field, select Field Modification. If you want to file a claim for multiple items, click on the link Click here for Multiple Field Modification Claim. You need to provide the following details on this page to submit a field modification claim:

  • Service Provide Name: It auto populates.

  • Field Modification Code: Enter a unique code that identifies this campaign. To know more about Field Modification Code, click here.

  • Work Order number: Enter your internal tracking number for this claim.

  • Click on the link Search Serial Number to view and select the items for which the field modification claim is to be filed. Enter the serial number of the items or first few digits of the serial number and click Search. Select the items from the list and click Select. You can also search an item by Model Number, Service Provider Number, End Customer information or Year.

  • Commercial Policy: Select his check box when the claim is out of warranty. The selection of commercial policy field while filing a claim will pick the set payment definition on the basis of which the different cost categories will appear on the claim page. For further information, click here.

  • Date of Repair: Use the option to specify the date on which the service campaign was performed on this equipment. You can also type the date manually in this field.

  • Request SMR: If you want to submit your claim for a review to your District Service Manager (DSM), then check this checkbox. This is also called pre-authorization. The claim, on submitting, will be sent to the district service manager associated with your dealership.

  • SMR Reason: This field is shown only when you check the Request SMR check-box. Use this drop-down field to specify the reason why you are submitting your claim for a review by the DSM.

After you have entered all relevant details in the above fields click on Continue >>. This will also save your claim in the Draft folder. You are now on Page 2 of 2 of the New Claim page.

 

This page is divided into various sections for ease of navigation. The fields under some sections on this page are hidden. To view the fields, click on the section header. This will expand the section showing all fields under it. Likewise, clicking on the section header of an expanded section collapses it hiding all fields under it.

 

Claim Details

This is a read-only section shown on top of Page 2 of 2. This section provides you the following information about the claim: Dealer, Claim Type, Date of Claim, Work Order Number, Date of Failure, Date of Repair, Request SMR and SMR Reason.

 

Servicing Location

This section provides you information regarding the servicing location of the customer.

 

Equipment Details

This is also a read-only section and will provide you with the following details about the equipment on which you are filing a claim:

  • Serial Number: Serial number of the equipment. This is a hyperlink to the equipment details page of the equipment. For further details on the equipment details page, click here.

  • Item Number: Item number of the equipment. This is a hyperlink to the Components pop-up window. For further details on the Components window, click here.

  • Item Description: A short description of the equipment. If the description is truncated, mouse over on the text to view the entire description.

  • Product: Product name of the equipment.

  • Model: Model of the equipment.

  • Hours in Service/Energy Units: The number of hours the equipment was in service till the date of failure.

  • Overall Warranty Start Date: Date when the warranty coverages commenced on this equipment. This is the earliest start date among all policies associated with this equipment. This field will appear only in the case of serialized machine claim.

  • Overall Warranty End Date: Date when this equipment will be out of warranty. This is the latest end date among all policies associated with this equipment. This field will appear only in the case of serialized machine claim.

  • Additional Attribute: Any additional information associated to the equipment will auto-populate. For further reference, click here.

Failure Details

This section captures relevant information regarding the failure in the equipment. You need to provide the following details here:

  • Causal Part: Specify the part number of the part which caused the failure. This field supports auto-complete. As you enter the causal part number in this field, the application prompts the possible values in a drop-down list. You can select the appropriate part number by clicking on it.

  • Causal Part Description: It auto-populates.

  • Click on the Fault Location link. You can see a tree like hierarchy of fault location on this pop-up. Select the fault location and click on Set Fault Location.

  • Fault Found: The fault found captures the nature of the failure that happened. Use this drop-down field to select the appropriate fault found in the equipment.

Alarm Code

Alarm Codes are used to give additional information about the product while filing a claim. To know more about alarm codes, click here.

  • Alarm Code: Select the name of the alarm code from the drop-down.

  • Description: A short description of the alarm code will auto-populate.

Service Information

In this section you can specify all the services that were performed for the claim.

 

Service Procedure

A service procedure is identified using the job-code associated with it. Just like a fault code, the job code allows easy identification of the labor performed on the equipment.

 

To specify a labor detail,

  • Click on the option.

  • The Select Service Procedures pop-up is shown. You can see a tree like structure of service procedures here.

  • Click on the icon in front of a service procedure to view the entire structure.

  • Check the check-box against the service procedure you want to select.

  • You can select multiple service procedures on this window.

  • Now click on the Add Service Procedure button. All service procedures selected by you will be shown as different rows under the Service Information section.

You can view/enter the following details for each service procedure:

  • Job Code: The service procedure selected by you. A mouse over on the icon alongside the job code will show a short description of the service procedure. There are few job codes that are related and cannot be selected. To know more about inclusive job codes, click here.

  • Standard Labor Hour(s): Time, in hours and minutes, required to perform the labor. This is the standard time required for the labor.

  • Additional Labor Hour(s): In addition to standard labor time, you have the option to enter additional labor hours, if any, for the labor. This utility is applicable to only those dealers who are added on the Additional Labor Eligibility page. Click here for further reference.

  • Reason for Additional Labor Hour(s): Specify the reason for consuming additional time for the labor. This field is shown only when you enter additional labor hours.

  • Additional Attribute: This is an optional field. If an attribute is associated to the equipment, you will see Enter Additional Attribute link where you can add any additional information here which may be text, date or number depending upon the data type selected while creating the attribute. For further reference, click here.

You can delete a service procedure by clicking the option corresponding that row.

 

Labor Split

Sometimes the repair/service may require labor to work beyond the normal working hours and hence eligible for overtime pay.

To enter the labor split information, click on the option.

  • Over Time Labor Split: Select the defined labor split from the drop-down.

  • Labor Hours(s): Enter the number of hours the labor was performed beyond the normal working hours.

  • Reason for Over Time Labor Hour(s): Enter the reason for performing the overtime labor hours.

You can delete a service procedure by clicking the option corresponding that row. For further reference, click here.

 

Components Replaced

The Components Replaced section has two sub-sections: Parts Replaced Installed and Outside Parts/Services.

 

Parts Replaced Installed

In this sub-section you can specify details on all components that were replaced for fixing the failure.

Note: It is mandatory that the causal part be a part of the Parts Replaced sub-section for a claim.

To enter details for a replaced part, click on the option. A new row is added to this sub-section. In a row you need to mention the following information for each part:

  • Removed Serial Number: If the replaced part was serialized, use this drop-down field to specify the serial number of the part. Once u select the serial number, you will see that the Removed Part Number and Installed Part Number fields will be automatically populated with the part number associated with the replaced part.

  • Removed Part Number: If the replaced part was non-serialized, then enter the part number of the part here. You will see that the Installed Part Number field will be automatically populated with the part number that you specify in this field.

  • Installed Serial Number: If you have removed a serialized part, then you would have replaced it with another serialized part of the same kind. Enter the serial number of the newly installed part in this field.

  • Installed Part Number: This field will be pre-populated with the part number of the removed part. You can over-write this if the part number of the newly installed part is different from that of the removed part.

  • Quantity: Enter the quantity of parts of the specified kind that needed replacement. You will notice that in case of serialized parts, the Quantity field will show the value 1 and will be disabled.

  • Description: This is a read-only field and will show a short description of the replaced part specified by you.

You can enter multiple parts at a time in this sub-section by clicking the option. To delete a row, click on the option corresponding to that row.

 

Outside Parts/Services

In this sub-section you can claim expenses related to any outside services or parts that the dealer used for fixing the failure. To enter details for a part/service, click on the option. A new row is added to this sub-section. In a row you need to mention the following information for each part/service:

  • Description: Provide a detailed description of the outside service/part that you are claiming.

  • Quantity: Enter the quantity for the service/part.

  • Unit Price: Enter the unit price for the service/part.

  • Invoice: For any outside part/service used, it is mandatory to provide an invoice as proof of expense. To attach an invoice,

  • Click on the Attach Invoice link. A pop-up window opens.

  • Use the Browse button to browse through your system for the soft-copy of the invoice.

  • After providing the proper link to the invoice file, click Attach. The selected invoice file will be attached to your claim.

To delete a row, click on the option.

 

Miscellaneous Parts

Apart from the parts available in the system, if you want to enter any new parts information, you can enter it under Miscellaneous Parts section. To know more about Miscellaneous Parts, click here.

  • Part Number: Enter the new part number.

  • Description: It auto populates.

  • Quantity: Enter the quantity for the part.

  • Unit Price: It auto populates.

  • UOM: The unit of measurement for the part will auto populate. Click here, for more information.

Miscellaneous

In this section, you can enter travel or any other related expenses incurred by the dealer:

  • Travel Distance: Enter the total distance travelled by the technicians for repair works. Use the drop-down field to specify the unit of measurement for the distance. The options available are Miles and KM.

  • Travel Location: Enter the complete address of the location travelled to by the technicians to complete the repair work.

  • Travel Hours: Enter the number of hours spent in travelling to the specified location.

  • Travel Trips: Enter the number of trips made to the specified location.

  • Technician:  Use the drop-down to specify the technician who performed this labor.

Similar to Travel Information, enter the other related expenses such as Item Freight and Duty, Parking and Toll expense, Meals, etc. under Incidentals.

 

Claim Description

This section allows you to provide a detailed description of the fault found in the equipment, cause for the failure of the equipment and the repair works performed to correct the failure.

  • Condition Found: Use this text field to provide a detailed description of the failure found in the equipment.

  • Work Performed: Use this text box to provide a detailed description of the repair work performed to correct the failure.

  • Claim Notes: This is an optional field. You can provide any additional comments like additional labor, travel involved in this text box.

Manage Documents

In this section you can attach binary documents, provided by the dealer, to support any of expenses and claims or the nature of failure in the equipment. To attach a document, click on the button. A pop-up window is shown. Click on the Browse button to browse through your system for the document. You can attach up to 20 documents for a claim. After you have mentioned the exact location of the documents, click Attach. All selected documents will be attached to the claim. To delete an entry, click on the option.

 

Actions

After providing necessary details in all mandatory fields, you can take one of the following actions on the claim:

  • Cancel: To close the New Claim page without saving any details entered on Page 2 of 2 after the last save.

  • Go to Page 1: To  go to Page 1 of 2. You can edit the data entered on page 1.

  • Delete: To delete this claim from the Draft folder.

  • Save: To save all details entered by you in Page 2 of 2. The claim will be saved in the Draft Claim folder.

  • Validate: To validate the claim and submit it. For further information about claim validation and submission, click here.

Depending on the action taken by you, a success message will be shown confirming your action. The message will hide itself and the current page will be closed.
