PENDING RECOVERY INITIATION

A supplier recovery initiator is a person who is required to kick off vendor recovery after a warranty claim is closed. The Pending Recovery Initiation inbox displays all the warranty claims that have been closed by the claims processor. Hence, as soon as the claims processor accepts a warranty claim, that claim moves into this folder of the supplier recovery initiator. This happens only if the claim is eligible for supplier recovery i.e., there is at least one part that has at least one recovery contract against it and it is not a commercial policy claim.

 

To view the Pending Recovery Initiation inbox,

  • Click on the Pending Recovery Initiation inbox under the Claims tab on the left hand pane.

  • Click on the Pending Recovery Initiation link under Action Folders from the Home Page.

Either of the above actions takes you to the Pending Recovery Initiation page. On this page, you can see a table that lists all claims which are accepted by the claims processor.

 

Double click on a claim to view the supplier contract update screen. From this page, you can view the decisions taken by the claim processor and the parts receiver through the Comments section. Here, the Adjust recovery claims button is displayed instead of the Submit button. Clicking this button takes you to the screen where you can make adjustments to the recovery claims. Thus this feature allows you to adjust the recovery amounts across various recovery claims.

 

Click Adjust recovery claims to display the screen with the following two sections:

 

Claim Details: This section displays the following details about the claim.

  • Claim number: This is a hyperlink to the read only view of the warranty claim.

  • Causal part: The part that caused the failure.

  • Status: The current status of the warranty claim.

Recovery Claims: This section lists out all the recovery claims created. The vendor claim number of each recovery claim is displayed as the header of the section (which is expandable-collapsible). Each recovery claim has the following information:

  • Parts: This lists all the parts that are on that recovery claim in a tabular structure with two columns: Part Number and Part Description.

  • Recovery details: This lists the recovery details in two columns: Details and Individual.

  • Details display the Supplier name, Supplier Contract and the Ship check box (whether the part had to be shipped or not). These are read only fields.

  • Individual displays the amount being recovered as a table with four columns which are editable:

  • Cost element: The cost category being claimed for (Parts, labor, travel etc.).

  • Warranty Claim Value: The warranty claim value of the claim.

  • Contract value: The value on the contract that is payable.

  • Actual value: The value on the warranty claim when it was finally processed.

From this section, you can adjust the recovery amounts across various recovery claims. You can perform any of the following action on this page:

  • Cancel: Click this button to close the page without saving the changes.

  • Save: Click this button to save the changes made.

  • Initiate Supplier Recovery: Click this button to kick off supplier recovery. This kicks off the vendor recovery and the claim is sent to the supplier's New inbox and the supplier recovery processor’s Awaiting Supplier Response inbox. Thereafter it is up to the supplier recovery processor to bring the vendor claim to closure.
