RELATED FIELD Product Improvement Management

Some campaigns are inherently included in others, where if one campaign is performed, there is no need to perform the other one. For instance consider two campaigns:

Campaign A: Service a Part.

Campaign B:  Remove, service and reinstall the part.

Hence, if a dealer has performed campaign B, then Campaign A is obviously performed as well. In this case, the dealer should not be permitted to claim for both campaigns. Campaign A and B are therefore related.

 

The administrator can create a related campaign record and perform actions such as delete and update the related campaign as well. Deleting a related campaign would remove the relationship between the campaigns contained in the related campaign, i.e., submitting a claim for one campaign will not remove the other (previously) related campaign on the unit.

 

To create a related campaign,

  • Click on Related Field Product Improvement Management under the Field Product Improvement folder listed under the Warranty Admin tab on the left pane. The page displays all the related campaigns.

  • Click on the Create Related Field Product Improvement button in the listing toolbar. It takes the user to a screen where the user can specify the related campaign details (code and description).

  • Click on the button to enter the related campaigns. The system makes it mandatory for the user to set up at least two campaigns in the related campaigns. The system displays an error message – “Please set up at least two campaigns for the relation”. This check is maintained at both creation and update of the related campaigns. If multiple campaigns are on a unit and some of them are related and a claim is “SUBMITTED” against any one of those related campaigns, the system should automatically remove all the other related campaigns on the unit.

  • Click Submit. Click Cancel to close this page without saving any details.

If a claim has been filed on a campaign related to one that is being newly added to a unit, the newly added campaign should be in-active. For example, consider two campaigns A and B related by related campaign R1. A is currently on a unit, but B isn't. A claim is filed on campaign A. The admin now adds campaign B to the unit. In this case, the system should make B inactive so that no claim can be filed against it. If the claim on A is denied, then both A and B should now be active on the unit.
