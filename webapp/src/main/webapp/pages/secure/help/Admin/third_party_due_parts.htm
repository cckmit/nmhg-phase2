THIRD PARTY DUE PARTS

The Third Party Due Parts folder lists all those parts, belonging to third party claims, which need to be returned. To know more about Third Party Claims, click here.

 

To view the parts that need to be returned to the OEM, click on the Part Returns tab on the left-hand pane. The tab will open to reveal the folders under it. Click on the Third Party Due Parts folder. The right-hand pane displays the Third Party Due Parts table that lists all parts due to be returned to the OEM. For further information on navigating through the table, see Lists.

 

You can select a view - Location View or Claim View, to view the data in the Third Party Due Parts table. The data will be re-arranged to reflect the view chosen by you.

  • Location View consolidates all the parts across the claims that are to be shipped to the same location. You can see all parts grouped by return locations, in a tabular format.

  • Claim View consolidates all parts belonging to one claim that must be returned to the OEM. The parts may have different return locations. You can see all parts belonging to individual claims, grouped together in a tabular format.

Double clicking on the record will open a detail page. The detail page is divided into two sections, Claim Details and Part Return Details, for ease of navigation. The Claim Details section displays information about the claim to which the part belongs. The Part Return Details show all the parts that are to be returned to a specified location from the same claim.

 

Under the Part Return Details section, enter the number of parts of the given specification that are to be shipped. Once you enter the comments, click on the Generate Shipment button to generate the shipment for that part. The part is now moved to the Shipment Generated folder. A success message will be shown confirming your action.
