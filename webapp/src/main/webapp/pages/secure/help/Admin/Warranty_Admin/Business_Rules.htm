business rules

The Warranty Management application allows you to create various business rules using the business conditions created by you. The rules allow you to manage the flow of a claim from submission to acceptance/denial.

 

You can create rules for,

 

Claim Processor Routing, Advisor Routing, CP Advisor Routing, Recovery Claim Processor Routing, Processor Authority - Claims filed by the service providers may be sent for manual adjudication to various processors or advisors or CP advisors or recovery claim processors. The Warranty Management application allows you to route these claims to specific processors or advisors or CP advisors by creating business rules.

 

Claim Processing - The Warranty Management system allows you to create rules for processing the claim submitted by the service providers. Based on the business conditions attached to a rule, you can send claims for manual adjudication, put claims on hold as well as deny claims automatically.

 

Entry Validations - The Warranty Management application allows you to create rules to be applied at the time of claim validations by service providers. When a service provider validates a claim before submission, the entry validation rules created by you will be applied on the claim. If a rule fails, depending on the severity of the business condition attached to the rule, either an error message or a warning message is shown to the service provider.

 

The service provider will not be allowed to submit the claim if the entry validation rule failure shows an error message. Only after the cause of error is rectified, will the claim be allowed to be submitted. On the other hand, if the rule failure shows a warning message, then the service provider is allowed to submit the claim, as is.

 

DSM Routing - Every dealership has a District Service Manager (DSM) who supervises its functioning. A service provider is allowed to send a claim to the DSM associated with the dealership, for reviews, before submitting the claim to the OEM. The Warranty Management application allows you to route these claims to specific DSMs by creating business rules.

 

Click on the Manage Business Rules folder in the Warranty Admin tab. The following links are shown here:

  • Claim Processor Routing

  • Recovery Claim Processor Routing

  • Claim Processing

  • Entry Validations

  • DSM Routing

  • Advisor Routing

  • Processor Authority

  • CP Advisor Routing

Click on an individual link to view and create a business rule. The Rules table on this page, lists all rules created by you. For information on navigating through this table, see Lists. Double click on the rule in the table. The <rule name> page is shown.

 

You can add new routing rules or edit existing rules as per your need.

 

To create a new business rule:

  • Click on the button in the listing toolbar.

  • The New Business Rule page is shown.

Note: If no user labels are set up, then an error message will be shown suggesting you to set up an user label before creating a routing rule.

  • In the Provide a phrase describing the business rule field, enter a phrase under which you want to save this rule.

  • In the Provide a phrase that will be displayed when the rule fails field, enter a phrase that should be displayed during the failure of this rule.

  • In the Search business conditions to create a rule field, enter a search phrase to search for business conditions.

  • Click Search. All business conditions that match your search phrase will be listed on the New Business Rule page.

  • Select the radio button corresponding to the business condition that you want to add to your rule.

  • In the Rule Group field, select a rule group from the drop down and assign this rule to the rule group. To know more about rule groups, click here.

  • In the Action field, you can see two drop-downs. The first drop-down has the assign options and the second drop-down lists the user labels.

  • Select an assign option from the drop-down. The options available are: Assign To User Group, Assign to LOA Scheme and Not Assign To. To know more about User Groups, click here. Click here to know more about LOA schemes.

  • If you want to clear the details entered by you, click Reset. All fields will be cleared.

  • To create the rule with the selected condition, click Add Business Rule. The rule will be created and displayed in the Rules table.

For example, create a business condition "If Date of Failure is after May 01, 2007". Create an user label, Users1 with Processor 1 and Processor 2. Now create the rule, "If Date of Failure is after May 01, 2007, then Assign To Users1". When this rule is applied to claims submitted by service providers, if the Date of Failure mentioned in the claim is after May 01, 2007, then all such claims will be assigned to either Processor 1 or Processor 2, based on load balancing.

 

Note: It is mandatory to create business conditions before creating rules. To know more about business conditions, click here. Also, before creating routing rules, it is mandatory to set up user labels along with business conditions. To know more about labels, click here.

You can edit any value in an existing rule by opening it in the detailed page.

 

To edit a rule,

  • Double click on a rule in the Rules table. It will open in the <expression name> page.

  • Edit any field on this page.

  • Click the Update Rule button to save the changes made by you.

To delete a rule, open it in the detailed page and click on the DeActivate Rule button. The rule will be removed from the Rules table.

 

If the claim is satisfying more than one business condition, then the system will check for the priority and route it to the processor. Manage Business Rule Groups helps in managing the rules by changing the priority of the business conditions. To know more about rule groups, click here.
