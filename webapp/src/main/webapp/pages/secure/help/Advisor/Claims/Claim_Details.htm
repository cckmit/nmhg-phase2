claim details

When you double click on a claim number in any folder or in the search results, the claim opens in the details page in a new page, <claim number>. Depending on the folder from which you are viewing the detail page, you are allowed to add/edit data on this page. If you have opened the detail page from a read-only folder, then you cannot perform any add/edit on this page.

 

Note: The data shown in the claim detail page depends on the current status of the claim. Hence you may or may not see all sections and all fields mentioned below for some claims.

 

The claim detail page is divided into various sections for ease of navigation. You can view the following sections in this page: Processing Errors, Notifications, Claim History, Claim Details, Equipment Details, Failure Information, Service Information, Components Replaced, Miscellaneous, Comments, Manage Documents, Payment Details, and Actions. The sections shown in the detail page depends on the folder from which you are viewing the page.

 

The fields under some sections on this page may be hidden. To view the fields, click on the section header. This will expand the section showing all fields under it. Likewise, clicking on the section header of an expanded section collapses it hiding all fields under it.

 

Claim History

This section displays the audit trail of the claim. It shows the following details for each of the update that happened on the claim in a tabular format:

  • Date and Time: The date and time when the update happened. Clicking on the date and time value will take you to the claim detail page that shows the details of the claim, as corresponding to this update, without the Claim History section.

  • Status: The status of the claim after that update happened.

  • User: Name of the user who performed the update.

  • Comments: Any comments specified for the claim during that update.

  • Internal Comments: Any internal comments specified for the claim during the update.

Data shown in this section is sorted in descending order of the date and time.

 

Claim Details

This section is read-only and shows the following details about the claim:

  • Claim Number: The claim number generated while filing the claim.

  • Claim Type: Kind of claim. The claim can be of type machine, parts or field modification.

  • Claim Status: The status of the claim.

  • Request SMR: If the dealer had sent this claim to the DSM for a review, then this check-box will be checked.

  • SMR Reason: The reason provided by the dealer for submitting the claim to the DSM for review.

  • Dealer: Name of the service provider who filed this claim.

  • Date of Claim: Date when this claim was submitted.

  • Date of Failure: Date when the equipment failed.

  • Date of Repair: Date when the equipment was repaired.

Servicing Location

This section provides you information regarding the servicing location of the customer.

 

Equipment Details

This is a read-only section. For a machine claim, this section displays the following details about the equipment on which this claim was submitted:

  • Serial Number: Serial number of the equipment. This is a hyperlink to the equipment details page of the equipment. For further details on the equipment details page, click here.

  • Item Number: Item number of the equipment.

  • Product: Product name of the equipment.

  • Model: Model of the equipment.

  • Hours in Service: The number of hours the equipment was in service till the date of failure.

  • Description: A short description of the equipment. If the description is truncated, mouse over on the text to view the entire description.

  • Startup Date: Date when this equipment was delivered to the end customer.

  • Warranty Start Date: Date when the warranty coverages commenced on this equipment. This is the earliest start date among all policies associated with this equipment.

  • Warranty End Date: Date when this equipment will be out of warranty. This is the latest end date among all policies associated with this equipment.

For a parts claim, the following details about the part on which this claim was submitted, are shown here:

  • Part Serial Number: Serial number of the part on which you are filing a claim.

  • Part Number: Part number of the part.

  • Product: Product name of the equipment in which this part was installed.

  • Model: Model of the equipment in which this part was installed.

  • Description: A short description of the part. If the description is truncated, mouse over on the text to view the entire description.

  • Date of Installation: Date when this part was installed in the equipment.

  • Warranty Start Date: Date when the warranty coverages commenced on this part. This is the earliest start date among all policies associated with this part.

  • Warranty End Date: Date when this part will be out of warranty. This is the latest end date among all policies associated with this part.

  • Equipment Serial Number: Serial number of the equipment in which this part was installed. This will a hyperlink to the equipment details page of that equipment. For further details on the equipment details page, click here.

Failure Information

You can see the following details about the failure that occurred in the equipment/part:

  • Causal Part Number/Causal Part Serial Number: Causal part details as entered by the dealer. If the causal part was serialized, then you can see the serial number of the part here. If it was a non-serialized part, then you can see the part number of that part here.

  • Causal Part Description: A short description of the causal part.

  • Fault Location: Failure location as entered by the dealer.

  • Fault Found: Fault identified in the equipment/part.

  • Failure Detail: Failure found in the equipment/part.

Service Information

In this section, you can see the following details about the services performed by the dealer to correct the fault in the equipment/part:

  • Job Code: The service procedure entered by the dealer. A mouse over on the icon alongside the job code will show a short description of the service procedure.

  • Service Technician: Name of the technician who performed the above mentioned labor.

  • Standard Labor Time: The standard labor time required to perform the above mentioned labor.

  • Additional Labor Time: Any additional labor specified by the dealer.

  • Reason for Additional Labor Time: Reason for using additional labor, as specified by the dealer.

  • Over Time Labor Split: Overtime labour, if any.

  • Labor Hours(s): The number of hours the labor performed beyond the normal working hours.

  • Reason for Over Time Labor Hour(s): Reason for performing the overtime labor hours.

Components Replaced

In the Parts Replaced sub-section, you can view the following details about each part replaced by the dealer during the repair work on the equipment:

  • Removed Part Serial Number: Serial number of the faulty part that was removed from the equipment. If the part was non-serialized, then you will see this field blank.

  • Removed Part Number: Part number of the faulty part that was removed from the equipment.

  • Installed Part Serial Number: Serial number of the part that was installed after removing the faulty part. If the part is non-serialized, then you will see this field blank.

  • Installed Part Number: Part number of the part that wad installed after removing the faulty part.

  • Quantity: Number of parts of the given specification that were replaced.

  • Description: A short description of the replaced part.

  • Unit Price: Cost price of the replaced part.

In the Outside Parts and Services sub-section, you can view the following details about any outside labor or part used by the dealer for repair:

  • Description: A short description of the service or part used by the dealer.

  • Unit Price: Cost price of the part or cost of service used by the dealer.

  • Invoice: Supporting invoices attached by the dealer. To view the invoice, click on its link. The Warranty Management system allows you to save the invoice to your desktop so that you can view it.

Miscellaneous

In the Incidentals sub-section you can view the following details about any travel related expenses cited by the dealer:

  • Travel Distance: Distance travelled by the technician for repair works, as mentioned by the dealer. The distance may either be in miles or in km units.

  • Travel Location Address: Complete postal address of the location travelled to by the technician. You can view the location on a map by clicking on the Google map icon alongside the address.

  • Travel Hours: Number of hours spent by the technician for traveling.

Manage Documents

Any documents attached by the dealer in support of the claimed reimbursements, will be found in this section. Click on the link of a document to download it to your desktop to view it.

 

Payment Details

In this section you can see complete details of the claim expenses broken down by different cost categories. The following details are shown as part of this section:

  • Cost Category: Name of the cost category.

  • Amount Asked: Amount asked by the service provider for this cost category.

  • Current Amount: Current claim amount for this cost category. this amount may or may not be equal to that claimed by the dealer.

For example, if you changed the labor hours for this claim, then the amount shown in the Current Amount field will be different than the amount shown in the Amount Asked field for Labor Hours cost category.

  • Percentage to Accept: The percentage of the total amount for this cost category accepted by you.

Memo Details

This section displays all the credit/debit memo generated for this claim. You can find the following details for each memo, sorted in descending order of the memo date, in a tabular format:

  • Memo Date: Date when the memo was generated.

  • Memo Number: Unique number assigned to this memo.

  • Memo Type: Kind of memo. The memo can be of type credit or debit.

  • Memo Amount: Amount associated with this demo.
