You can submit a parts claim when the equipment is out of warranty but the failed part is still covered by individual part warranty.

 

Enter the following fields to submit a parts claim:

  • Part Serial Number: Since you are filing a claim for non-serialized part, this files will be non-editable.

  • Part Number: Enter the part number of the part on which you are submitting a claim.

  • Description: A short description of the part will auto populate.

  • Specify Model: Enter the model name of the part.

  • Hours on Part: Enter the number of hours the part was in operation.

  • Hours in Service/Energy Units: Enter the number of hours the part has run till the date of failure.

  • Installation Date: Enter the date when this part was installed in the equipment.

  • Work Order Number: Enter your internal tracking number for the claim.

  • Date of Failure: Use the  option to specify the date on which the part failed. You can also type the date manually in this field.

  • Date of Repair: Use the  option to specify the date on which the part was repaired. You can also type the date manually in this field.

  • Commercial Policy: Select his check box when the claim is out of warranty. The selection of commercial policy field while filing a claim will pick the set payment definition on the basis of which the different cost categories will appear on the claim page. For further information, click here.

  • Request SMR: If you want to submit your claim for a review by your District Service Manager (DSM), then check this checkbox. This is also called pre-authorization. The claim, on submitting, will be sent to the district service manager associated with your dealership.

  • SMR Reason: This field is shown only when you check the Request SMR check-box. Use this drop-down field to specify the reason why you are submitting your claim for a review by the DSM.

After you have entered all relevant details in the above fields click on Continue >> to proceed to the next step. This will also save your claim in the Draft folder. You are now on Page 2 of 2 of the New Claim page. This page is divided into various sections for ease of navigation. The fields under some sections on this page are hidden. To view the fields, click on the section header. This will expand the section showing all fields under it. Likewise, clicking on the section header of an expanded section collapses it hiding all fields under it.

 

Claim Details

This is a read-only section shown on top of Page 2 of 2. This section provides you information about the claim entered by you on page 1. Click on Enter Owner Information link to select/enter the owner details. A pop-up window will appear. This window will list all existing customers of the selected type, who are already registered in your address book. If you want to create a new customer, then click on the Create Customer button on this window. You will be taken to the Create Customer page. For further information on creating new customers, click here.

 

Servicing Location

This section provides you information regarding the servicing location of the customer.

 

Failure Information

This section captures relevant information regarding the failure in the equipment. The information allows easy identification of the defective part in equipment. You need to provide the following details here:

  • Causal Part Number: It auto-populates.

  • Casual Part Description: It auto populates.

Alarm Code

Alarm Codes are used to give additional information about the product while filing a claim. To know more about alarm codes, click here.

  • Alarm Code: Select the name of the alarm code from the drop-down.

  • Description: A short description of the alarm code will auto-populate.

Labor Information

Total Labor Hours: Enter the total labor hours spent on the part/service.

 

Components Replaced

The Components Replaced section has two sub-sections: Parts Replaced Installed and Outside Parts/Services.

 

Parts Replaced Installed

In this sub-section you can specify details on all components that were replaced for fixing the failure.

Note: It is mandatory that the causal part be a part of the Parts Replaced sub-section for a claim.

To enter details for a replaced part, click on the option. A new row is added to this sub-section. In a row you need to mention the following information for each part:

  • Removed Serial Number: This field would appear blank as we are filing a claim for non-serialized part. The serial number of the removed part will auto populate if entered on page 1.

  • Removed Part Number: This field is pre-filled with the causal part number entered under failure information.

  • Installed Serial Number: If you have removed a non-serialized part, then you would have replaced it with another non-serialized part of the same kind, so this field would appear blank.

  • Installed Part Number: This field will be pre-populated with the part number of the removed part. You can over-write this if the part number of the newly installed part is different from that of the removed part.

  • Quantity: Enter the quantity of parts of the specified kind that needed replacement. You will notice that in case of serialized parts, the Quantity field will show the value 1 and will be disabled.

  • Description: This is a read-only field and will show a short description of the replaced part specified by you.

You can enter multiple parts at a time in this sub-section by clicking the option. To delete a row, click on the option corresponding to that row.

 

Outside Parts/Services

In this sub-section you can claim expenses related to any outside services or parts that the dealer used for fixing the failure. To enter details for a part/service, click on the option. A new row is added to this sub-section. In a row you need to mention the following information for each part/service:

  • Description: Provide a detailed description of the outside service/part that you are claiming.

  • Quantity: Enter the quantity for the service/part.

  • Unit Price: Enter the unit price for the service/part.

  • Invoice: For any outside part/service used, it is mandatory to provide an invoice as proof of expense. To attach an invoice,

  • Click on the Attach Invoice link. A pop-up window opens.

  • Use the Browse button to browse through your system for the soft-copy of the invoice.

  • After providing the proper link to the invoice file, click Attach. The selected invoice file will be attached to your claim.

To delete a row, click on the option.

 

Miscellaneous Parts

Apart from the parts available in the system, if you want to enter any new parts information, you can enter it under Miscellaneous Parts section. To know more about Miscellaneous Parts, click here.

  • Part Number: Enter the new part number.

  • Description: It auto populates.

  • Quantity: Enter the quantity for the part.

  • Unit Price: It auto populates.

  • UOM: The unit of measurement for the part will auto populate. Click here, for more information.

  • Threshold Quantity: The threshold quantity set for the part will auto populate.

Miscellaneous

In this section, you can enter travel or any other related expenses incurred by the dealer:

  • Travel Distance: Enter the total distance travelled by the technicians for repair works. Use the drop-down field to specify the unit of measurement for the distance. The options available are Miles and KM.

  • Travel Location: Enter the complete address of the location travelled to by the technicians to complete the repair work.

  • Travel Hours: Enter the number of hours spent in travelling to the specified location.

  • Travel Trips: Enter the number of trips made to the specified location.

  • Technician:  Use the drop-down to specify the technician who performed this labor.

Similar to Travel Information, enter the other related expenses such as Item Freight and Duty, Parking and Toll expense, Meals, etc. under Incidentals.

 

Claim Description

This section allows you to provide a detailed description of the fault found in the equipment, cause for the failure of the equipment and the repair works performed to correct the failure.

  • Condition Found: Use this text field to provide a detailed description of the failure found in the equipment.

  • Work Performed: Use this text box to provide a detailed description of the repair work performed to correct the failure.

  • Claim Notes: This is an optional field. You can provide any additional comments like additional labor, travel involved in this text box.

Manage Documents

In this section you can attach binary documents, provided by the dealer, to support any of expenses and claims or the nature of failure in the equipment. To attach a document, click on the button. A pop-up window is shown. Click on the Browse button to browse through your system for the document. You can attach up to 20 documents for a claim. After you have mentioned the exact location of the documents, click Attach. All selected documents will be attached to the claim. To delete an entry, click on the option.

 

Actions

After providing necessary details in all mandatory fields, you can take one of the following actions on the claim:

  • Cancel: To close the New Claim page without saving any details entered on Page 2 of 2 after the last save.

  • Go to Page 1: To  go to Page 1 of 2. You can edit the data entered on page 1.

  • Delete: To delete this claim from the Draft folder.

  • Save: To save all details entered by you in Page 2 of 2. The claim will be saved in the Draft Claim folder.

  • Validate: To validate the claim and submit it. For further information about claim validation and submission, click here.

Depending on the action taken by you, a success message will be shown confirming your action. The message will hide itself and the current page will be closed.
